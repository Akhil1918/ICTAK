Dune

Frank Herbert

About the Book

Dune is a groundbreaking science fiction epic that explores complex themes of politics, religion, ecology, and human potential. Set on the desert planet Arrakis, the story follows Paul Atreides, whose family accepts the stewardship of this dangerous world. The novel delves into a future where noble houses control planetary fiefs, and the most valuable substance in the universe—the spice melange—can only be found on Arrakis.

Book Details

  • Genre: Science Fiction
  • Published: 1965
  • Pages: 412
  • Awards: Hugo Award, Nebula Award
